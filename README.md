# Global Air Crash Analysis (1908–2024)

## Overview
Excel data analysis project covering 116 years of global air crash incidents. Built pivot tables, interactive slicer-linked charts, and a KPI dashboard to answer 10 research questions on crash trends, fatalities, and risk factors.

## Tools Used
Microsoft Excel (pivot tables, charts, slicers, dashboard design)

## Dataset
- **Source:** Kaggle — Air Crashes Full Data 1908–2024
- **Size:** 5,036 records, 116 years (1908–2024)

## What I Did
- Verified data integrity (no blanks/dashes in key numeric columns) and engineered a `Survivors` column (`Aboard − Fatalities`) to enable survival-rate analysis.
- Built 8 pivot tables summarizing crashes by year, country, manufacturer, month, quarter, and operator.
- Created 6 interactive charts (line, bar, column, pie) linked to pivot tables with slicers for filtering.
- Assembled a dedicated dashboard sheet with KPI cards, all 6 charts, and a consistent color theme.

## Key Findings
- Fatalities peaked in 1972 at 2,796 and declined to 86 by 2023, reflecting major improvements in aviation safety, aircraft technology, and regulation.
- Russia recorded the highest total fatalities (8,113), driven by its large military fleet and Soviet-era incidents; Brazil (2,898) and Colombia (2,666) followed, reflecting the challenge of aviation over complex terrain.
- December was the highest-fatality month (10,910) and Q3 (Jul–Sep) the most dangerous quarter, with over 31,000 cumulative fatalities nearly a third higher than the safest month, April.
